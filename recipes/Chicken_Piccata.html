Chicken Piccata

Description

an Italian-American dish featuring thinly pounded or sliced chicken cutlets, dredged in flour, saut√©ed, and served with a lemon-butter sauce, often including capers.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup capers, drained
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • Cooked pasta or mashed potatoes (for serving)

Instructions:

  • Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  • Place the flour in a shallow dish and lightly dredge each chicken breast in the flour, shaking off any excess.
  • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  • Once hot, add the chicken breasts and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Remove chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  • In the same skillet, add the butter and allow it to melt, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  • Pour in the lemon juice, chicken broth, and white wine (if using), stirring to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  • Add the capers and cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce has reduced slightly.
  • Return the chicken breasts to the skillet and cook for another 2 minutes, spooning some of the sauce over the chicken.
  • Remove the chicken from the skillet and place on serving plates.
  • Pour the sauce and capers over the chicken and garnish with fresh parsley.
  • Serve with pasta, mashed potatoes, or your preferred side dish.
